Center Types

Differentiated Hub (H-type) and Local (L-type) Digital Technology Education Centers

The digital technology education centers established in this project are classified into two types according to their functional hierarchy and roles: KOICA Al-Khwarizmi H(Hub)-type and KOICA IT Village L(Local)-type. Hub-type centers supervise educational planning, ToT, and high-level training operations, while Local-type centers promote grassroots digital literacy and regional accessibility.

H-type (Hub) vs L-type (Local) Center Comparison

Parameter KOICA Al-Khwarizmi H-type (Hub) KOICA IT Village L-type (Local)
Concept & Role Acts as a regional hub supervising IT educational planning, instructor training (ToT), advanced courses, and managing nearby small local town centers. Focuses on remote youth digital literacy, providing basic and mid-level IT education to overcome accessibility barriers.
Quantity 2 centers (Nukus IT Park in Karakalpakstan, Karshi IT Park in Kashkadarya) 14 centers (Distributed across target local towns)
Method Remodeling and modernization of existing IT Park buildings. New construction of standard 1-story buildings.
Gross Area • Nukus Hub: ~550㎡ (1 floor)
• Karshi Hub: ~1,100㎡ (2 floors)		 ~525㎡ per center (standardized footprint)
Curriculum Scope Digital transformation (DX), AI/Big Data intro, IoT development, server programming, and advanced career support. Digital literacy basics, computer applications (Office suite), basic programming algorithms and coding.
Key Characteristics • Promotes youth employment & advanced IT ecosystem.
• Co-managed by PIU and resident PMC experts for sustainability.
• Supervises IT certification standards and monitoring. 		• Bridging regional digital divide.
• Standardized classroom environments, textbooks, and equipment.
• Closely linked with the respective regional Hub center.

⚙️ IT Equipment Procurement & Standard Distribution (Activity 1.2)

Procures and distributes necessary furniture, fixtures, and advanced IT training equipment tailored to each center's hardware scale, space programs, and curricula.

💼 Key Implementation & Procurement Process
  • PMC (PMC Team) Role: Reviews the quantity, specifications, and appropriateness of the equipment procurement plan submitted by the PIU, drafting a review opinion letter for the KOICA office. PMC also supervises on-site installation inspections led by IT experts.
  • PIU (Recipient Agency) Role: Prepares the list of necessary equipment, technical specs, budget estimates, and layouts per space program, executing the procurement directly in consultation with KOICA.
  • Recipient Country Procurement System: Carried out in accordance with the host country's official bidding system. An administrative lead time of about 3 to 4 months is expected from bidding to delivery.
⏳ Procurement Schedule & Contingencies
  • Timeline:
    • Equipment Procurement Plan Support: 2026 Q1 ~ 2028 Q1 (24 months)
    • Equipment Installation & Inspection: 2027 Q2 ~ 2028 Q4 (21 months)
  • Priority on Hub (H-type) Centers: Equipment is supplied first to the KOICA Al-Khwarizmi Hub-type centers (Nukus, Karshi) to support textbook development and instructor ToT training, then expanded to the 14 L-type IT Villages.
  • Contingency Plan for Educational Delivery: If remodeling of H-type centers is delayed, a **Basic PC Lab will be set up early** at a temporary location to ensure curriculum development and training can proceed on schedule.
