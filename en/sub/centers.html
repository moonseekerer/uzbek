Center Types

Differentiated Hub (H-type) and Local (L-type) Digital Technology Education Centers

The digital technology education centers established in this project are classified into two types according to their functional hierarchy and roles: KOICA Al-Khwarizmi H(Hub)-type and KOICA IT Village L(Local)-type. Hub-type centers supervise educational planning, ToT, and high-level training operations, while Local-type centers promote grassroots digital literacy and regional accessibility.

Hub Type

KOICA Al-Khwarizmi H-type

  • Scale: Established inside the IT PARK of Kashkadarya and Karakalpakstan regions (2 centers in total)
  • Method: Remodeling of existing buildings (linked with the Al-Khwarizmi program)
  • Timeline: Year 2 of the project
  • Purpose: Research & supervision of educational contents, overall management of regional projects, leading regional standard environment setups, and monitoring outcomes
  • Entity: Recipient Government and PMC (joint operations during the project period)
  • Key Activities:
    - Establish standard layouts and equipment specifications by type
    - Develop standard curricula including IT basics, IoT, and AI education
    - Develop and verify IT educational programs suitable for Levels 1 to 5
    - Develop and verify courses on new technologies and lead regional specialties
    - Train IT mentors (ToT) and operate advanced education courses
    - Plan and operate regular/weekend courses
    - Plan/host/support career-linking activities and events
  • Characteristics: Established early to supervise operations. Resident experts provide sustainable operational management and technical support.
Local Type

KOICA IT Village L-type

  • Scale: 14 centers in total in target districts of Kashkadarya and Karakalpakstan
  • Method: New construction of 1-story buildings (linked with the IT Village program)
  • Timeline: Year 3 of the project
  • Purpose: Educational operations using standardized environments, textbooks, and mentors
  • Entity: Recipient Government and PMC (joint operations during the project period)
  • Key Activities:
    - Build 14 new centers based on regional demands and priorities
    - Conduct educational activities and report outcomes using supplied IT textbooks and equipment
    - Autonomous curriculum operations reflecting regional environments and youth needs
  • Characteristics:
    - Improve IT education accessibility for remote youth
    - Secure standardized IT competencies using standard education programs
    - Focus on digital literacy improvement for remote youth
    - Expand ICT education foundation, improve regional environment with limited resources
    - Link closely with H-type centers in each region to produce educational progress and outcomes

🏫 H(Hub)-type Education Center Room Specs & Capacity Layout

Serving as the core educational node for each region, the Hub center consists of about 10 advanced functional rooms. Each room is optimally designed to balance learning efficiency, equipment accessibility, and user convenience.

Multi-purpose Classroom

Space for basic theory, language learning, and major academic lectures. Sizes are diversified to fit class sizes, with a maximum capacity of 40 students.

IT Lab (Computer Room)

Recognizing the lack of digital device exposure among youth, two computer labs with 20-student capacities are built to guarantee mid-level IT education.

IoT & Client Server Lab

Equipped with high-spec PCs and practice software for advanced practical trainings in IoT hardware programming and client-server configurations.

E-Lounge

An open electronic library concept for study and leisure. Provides barrier-free digital device exposure and self-directed learning environments.

Event Hall & Auditorium

A spacious hall for networking and talent matching events. Employs movable furniture to maximize multi-purpose flexibility within remodeling limits.

Office

Space for curriculum planning and administrative tasks. Designed to support 20-28 resident staff working simultaneously across different roles.

KOICA Room

Used as the local office for the KOICA PMC during the project period. It will be repurposed for general center operations and seminars post-project.

Meeting & Seminar Rooms

Features modular tables that can be joined or separated instantly based on group size. Oberserves a minimum of 2 rooms to facilitate team collaborations.

Restrooms (Accessible)

Strictly separated male and female spaces, with at least 1 disabled restroom built to guarantee a discrimination-free, fully accessible facility.
