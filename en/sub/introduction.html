Project Introduction

Inclusive Growth Partnership for the Digital Future of Uzbekistan

This project is supported by the Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA) to establish IT educational infrastructure in remote areas of Uzbekistan, specifically Kashkadarya and the Republic of Karakalpakstan. We train local instructors and support youth (including women and disabled youth) to secure jobs, launch startups, and enter IT-related university departments.

📋 Basic Project Info

  • • Project Name: Project for Promoting Employment and Entrepreneurship Capability through Improving the Youth IT Education Ecosystem in Uzbekistan
  • • Project Period: 2026 ~ 2032 (7-year project)
  • • Total Budget: 19,320 million KRW ($14 million)

📍 Target Regions & Infrastructure

  • • Country: Republic of Uzbekistan
  • • Regions: Kashkadarya Region, Republic of Karakalpakstan
  • • Beneficiaries: Local youth (including women and disabled youth)
  • • Centers: 16 KOICA Youth Digital Technology Education Centers across 16 districts (2 Hub-type remodeled, 14 Local-type built)

Background

- Aligns with 'Digital Uzbekistan 2030' and 'Uzbekistan 2030 Strategy' to expand youth IT education.
- Promotes synergy with Uzbekistan's 'Successors of Muhammad al-Khwarizmi' program.
- Resolves the digital infrastructure and teacher shortage gaps in 16 remote districts.

Objectives

- Infrastructure Expansion: Create eco-friendly IT educational environments to enhance digital access.
- Local Empowerment: Develop advanced SW curricula and train 103 local teachers (ToT) for sustainability.
- Career Linkage: Establish pathways for employment, technology-based startups, and university admission.

Annual Implementation Plan

Year 1 (2026) - Budget: 300 million KRW

Conduct baseline survey and digital education feasibility studies, form the Project Implementation Unit (PIU), establish construction plans, conduct policy invitation training in Korea for policymakers, and begin developing standard IT curricula.

Year 2 (2027) - Budget: 5,972.6 million KRW

Select builders and initiate construction of centers, procure educational hardware, conduct instructor training (ToT) for local mentors, and hold Korea invitation training for master mentors.

Year 3 (2028) - Budget: 6,056.8 million KRW

Complete construction and open the 16 Hub and Local centers, supply additional equipment, run pilot classes for 150 students, and initiate career mentoring and consulting workshops.

Years 4~6 (2029~2031) - Budget: ~5,278 million KRW

Perform midterm evaluations, scale up class sizes to 1,200 students per year, hold annual IT hackathons and job fairs, match students with internships, and organize university IT department tours.

Year 7 (2032) - Budget: 1,712.6 million KRW

Conduct endline evaluations, transfer center management and budget operations to the local government for self-reliance, and hold final output sharing seminars.
