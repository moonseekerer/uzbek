Project Introduction

Inclusive Growth Partnership for the Digital Future of Uzbekistan

This project is supported by the Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA) to establish IT educational infrastructure in remote areas of Uzbekistan, specifically Kashkadarya and the Republic of Karakalpakstan. We train local instructors and support youth (including women and disabled youth) to secure jobs, launch startups, and enter IT-related university departments.

📋 Basic Project Info

  • • Project Name: Project for Promoting Employment and Entrepreneurship Capability through Improving the Youth IT Education Ecosystem in Uzbekistan
  • • Project Period: 2026 ~ 2032 (7-year project)
  • • Total Budget: 19,320 million KRW ($14 million)

📍 Target Regions & Infrastructure

  • • Country: Republic of Uzbekistan
  • • Regions: Kashkadarya Region, Republic of Karakalpakstan
  • • Beneficiaries: Local youth (including women and disabled youth)
  • • Centers: 16 KOICA Youth Digital Technology Education Centers across 16 districts (2 Hub-type remodeled, 14 Local-type built)

Background

- Aligns with 'Digital Uzbekistan 2030' and 'Uzbekistan 2030 Strategy' to expand youth IT education.
- Promotes synergy with Uzbekistan's 'Successors of Muhammad al-Khwarizmi' program.
- Resolves the digital infrastructure and teacher shortage gaps in 16 remote districts.

Objectives

- Infrastructure Expansion: Create eco-friendly IT educational environments to enhance digital access.
- Local Empowerment: Develop advanced SW curricula and train 103 local teachers (ToT) for sustainability.
- Career Linkage: Establish pathways for employment, technology-based startups, and university admission.

Impact (Long-term Impact)
Narrative Summary
Contribute to inclusive economic growth by strengthening the digital competency of youth in remote areas and expanding youth employment and entrepreneurship (SDG 4.4)
Means of Verification
Uzbekistan government official digital indicators and national employment statistics reports
Important Assumptions
The political and economic situation and digital/youth employment policies of Uzbekistan remain stable.
Outcome (Medium-term Outcomes)
Narrative Summary
Improved IT education accessibility and career capability of youth in remote areas
Indicators & Verification
• Instructor & Student Competency: Pre/post-evaluation verify reports.
Important Assumptions
Uzbek government expansion support, and selected personnel participate stably without high dropout rates.
Outputs (Detailed Output Components)
Output 1. Improved IT Education Infrastructure Capacity (Building & Equipment)
Details
• 1.1.1 2 KOICA Al-Khwarizmi Hub-type centers remodeled
• 1.1.2 14 KOICA IT Village Local-type centers newly built
• 1.2.1/1.2.2 IT educational equipment supplied and installed for Hub (2) and Local (14) centers
Indicators & Verification
• Completion approval documents (MDT)
• Construction completion reports
• Equipment inspection reports & ownership transfer confirmations
Important Assumptions
• Completed centers are strictly utilized for IT education without being repurposed
• Instructors secure operational capacity and actively utilize hardware in classes
Output 2. Strengthened IT Education Institutional Capacity (Standard Model & Curricula)
Details
• 2.1.1 Standard operating model and guidelines developed (1 type in English, Uzbek, Karakalpak)
• 2.2.1 Track-based IT curricula developed and updated (17 types in English, Uzbek, Karakalpak)
• 2.3.1 Educational materials developed and distributed (17 types in Uzbek, Karakalpak)
Indicators & Verification
• Operating model establishment reports
• Curriculum development reports
• Textbook production and distribution records
Important Assumptions
• Ministry of Digital Technologies (MDT) accepts the model as official guideline standard
• Developed curricula align with local youth levels and regional labor market demands
Output 3. Developed IT Education Human Resource Capacity (ToT & Youth Programs)
Details
• 3.1.1/2/3 Master mentor invitation training (26), local training (26 * 2 times), general instructor local training (416 * 6 times)
• 3.2.1/2 Policy maker invitation training (10), center manager local training (48 * 5 times)
• 3.3.1 Career and startup program beneficiaries (660 youth, over 20% from vulnerable groups)
• 3.4.1/2/3/4 Digital assessment tool development & student mapping, graduates trained (4,950, over 20% vulnerable groups), monitoring loops (8 times)
Indicators & Verification
• Invitation and local training certificates issued
• Participant rosters & satisfaction survey reports
• Baseline/midline/endline reports & IT training certificate records
Important Assumptions
• Training outcomes are officially archived to prevent loss during organizational reshuffles
• Trainees maintain long-term career motivation and respond to tracer surveys

🔵 Korean Inputs (KOICA)
• Total Budget: $14.0 million (approx. 19.3 billion KRW)
• Project Period: 2026 ~ 2032 (7 years)
• Specialists Sent:
  - Project Manager (PM), Field Manager (FM), Project Assistant Officer (PAO)
  - ODA Performance Management Expert
  - Digital Literacy & Technology Education Expert
  - IT Center Operation & Administration Expert
  - Architecture Remodeling & IT Equipment Procurement Expert
🟢 Uzbek Inputs (MDT / DEDC)
• Site Selection & Provision: Provide land for H-type and L-type centers
• Construction Bidding: Manage local building contracts and completion approvals (PBA method)
• Equipment Procurement: Manage bidding, tax-free customs clearance, transport, and ownership transfer
• Target Recruitment: Recruit instructors, trainees, and youth students
• Administrative Support: Support expert dispatch administrative procedures
⚠️ Pre-conditions
• Title transfer and site provision by the host country must occur before project initiation
• Detailed agreements on scope, staffing, and reporting must be finalized between KOICA and MDT
• Tax exemptions (zero-VAT customs clearance) for imported equipment must be processed on time
• Local budget commitments for utility lines (power, fiber-optic) and staffing costs of the completed centers must be secured

Annual Implementation Plan

Year 1 (2026) - Budget: 300 million KRW

Conduct baseline survey and digital education feasibility studies, form the Project Implementation Unit (PIU), establish construction plans, conduct policy invitation training in Korea for policymakers, and begin developing standard IT curricula.

Year 2 (2027) - Budget: 5,972.6 million KRW

Select builders and initiate construction of centers, procure educational hardware, conduct instructor training (ToT) for local mentors, and hold Korea invitation training for master mentors.

Year 3 (2028) - Budget: 6,056.8 million KRW

Complete construction and open the 16 Hub and Local centers, supply additional equipment, run pilot classes for 150 students, and initiate career mentoring and consulting workshops.

Years 4~6 (2029~2031) - Budget: ~5,278 million KRW

Perform midterm evaluations, scale up class sizes to 1,200 students per year, hold annual IT hackathons and job fairs, match students with internships, and organize university IT department tours.

Year 7 (2032) - Budget: 1,712.6 million KRW

Conduct endline evaluations, transfer center management and budget operations to the local government for self-reliance, and hold final output sharing seminars.
